# CO 725 — Going concern, overindebtedness, restructuring plan (revised 2023)

The 2023 revision of CO 725 / 725a / 725b sharpened director duties around going concern and overindebtedness.

## Three layers

### 1. Liquidity (CO 725)
Directors monitor solvency. If the company cannot meet its short-term obligations:
- Must take measures to restore liquidity
- Must call a general meeting if necessary
- If unable, must file with the bankruptcy judge

### 2. Capital loss (CO 725a — "Kapitalverlust")
If the balance sheet shows that **half of share capital + legal reserves is no longer covered** by assets:
- Convene a general meeting **without delay**
- Propose measures (recapitalization, debt forgiveness, restructuring)
- Auditor must review the intermediate balance sheet (if any)

### 3. Overindebtedness (CO 725b — "Überschuldung")
If there is **founded concern** that liabilities exceed assets:
- Intermediate balance sheet at going-concern value AND liquidation value
- If both show overindebtedness:
  - Notify the bankruptcy judge **without delay** (overindebtedness notice)
  - Unless subordinations or restructuring plan demonstrate recovery within reasonable time (max ≈ 6 months by ESTV practice)

## Auditor's role

For ordinary audit (CO 727):
- Identify CO 725 risks during planning (PS 570 going concern)
- If overindebtedness apparent and directors fail to act: auditor must file the notice (CO 728c)

For limited audit (CO 727a):
- Similar duty under CO 729c — auditor calls attention to it in their report

## Restructuring plan

A credible plan must:
- Quantify the gap (how much capital injection needed)
- Identify funding sources (shareholders, banks, subordinations)
- Provide a realistic timeline (typically 6 months)
- Be endorsed by the board

If the plan is sound, the overindebtedness notice can be deferred (art. 725b al. 4).
