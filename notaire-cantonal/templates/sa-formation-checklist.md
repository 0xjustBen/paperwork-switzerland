# SA / AG formation — checklist (CO 620ss)

## Required documents

- [ ] Statuts (Statuten / Articles of association) — draft
- [ ] Acte constitutif (Gründungsurkunde) — drafted by notary
- [ ] Stampa (déclaration Stampa) — confirming no apports en nature undisclosed
- [ ] Lex Friedrich / Lex Koller declaration if real estate involved
- [ ] Bank attestation of paid-in capital (Kapitaleinzahlungsbescheinigung)
- [ ] Acceptance of mandate by board members, signatures certified
- [ ] Acceptance of mandate by auditor (or opting-out resolution if eligible)
- [ ] Domicile address (registered office)
- [ ] UID request (after registration)

## Capital requirements

- Minimum: **CHF 100,000**
- Paid-in: at least 20 %, minimum CHF 50,000 (whichever is greater)
- Currency: CHF (or foreign currency since 1.1.2023 reform — art. 621 al. 2 CO)

## Steps

1. Draft statuts (objet, capital, droits de vote, structure)
2. Open capital deposit account at a Swiss bank
3. Shareholders deposit capital
4. Notary executes acte constitutif (founders sign, board accepts)
5. Bank releases capital after Registry confirms registration
6. Filing with the cantonal commercial register (Handelsregisteramt)
7. Publication in Feuille officielle suisse du commerce (FOSC / SHAB)
8. UID issued by Federal Statistical Office (BFS)
9. VAT registration if turnover ≥ CHF 100,000 (art. 10 LTVA)

## Approximate fees (varies per canton)

- Notary fees: CHF 1,500 – 3,000 (depending on canton, Latin systems higher)
- Registry fees: CHF 600 – 1,200
- FOSC / SHAB publication: ~CHF 100
- Total typical: **CHF 2,000 – 5,000**

## Common pitfalls

- Apports en nature must be disclosed and audited (Stampa)
- Foreign founders need a Swiss-resident board member (or use professional director)
- Statuts in language of canton (FR / DE / IT) — not English officially
