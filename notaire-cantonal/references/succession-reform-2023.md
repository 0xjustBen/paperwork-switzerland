# Succession reform — 2023

The Swiss inheritance law was reformed effective **1 January 2023**. Key changes affect compulsory shares (réserves / Pflichtteile).

## Before 2023 vs from 2023

| Heir | Compulsory share before 2023 | From 1.1.2023 |
|------|------------------------------|---------------|
| Descendants | 3/4 of legal share | **1/2 of legal share** |
| Surviving spouse / registered partner | 1/2 of legal share | 1/2 of legal share (unchanged) |
| Parents | 1/2 of legal share | **abolished** (no longer compulsory) |

## Effect on freely disposable portion (quotité disponible)

| Heirs present | Freely disposable share — before | From 2023 |
|---------------|----------------------------------|-----------|
| Spouse + descendants | 3/8 | **1/2** |
| Descendants only | 1/4 | **1/2** |
| Spouse only | 1/2 | 1/2 (unchanged) |

## Implications

- **More planning flexibility**: the testator can now favor third parties (cohabiting partner, charity, godchild) up to half the estate in most family configurations.
- **Cohabiting partner** still has no legal inheritance right but receives more easily.
- **Existing wills/inheritance pacts**: still valid, but may now leave a larger freely disposable share — review recommended.
- **Donations**: the reform extends to lifetime gifts (Schenkungen) where they impinge on compulsory shares.

## Cantonal succession tax

Most cantons exempt:
- Spouse / registered partner: exempt in all cantons
- Direct descendants: exempt in 24/26 cantons (exceptions: VD, NE for distant lines)

Collateral (siblings, nieces/nephews, friends): progressive cantonal tax, often 10-50 %.
