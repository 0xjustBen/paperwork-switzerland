# Rent increase workflow

## Legal basis

- **CO 269**: forbidden if rent already abusive
- **CO 269a**: presumption of non-abuse — increase justified by:
  - lett. a: cost-of-living index change
  - lett. b: reference mortgage rate increase
  - lett. c: increase in building maintenance costs
  - lett. d: value-adding works (capitalizable)
  - lett. e: bringing rent to a level customary in the locality and neighborhood
- **CO 269d**: official cantonal form mandatory; signed by landlord; states the increase amount, the new rent, the new starting date, the reason, and the appeal procedure
- **CO 270b**: tenant's right to challenge within **30 days** at the conciliation authority

## Required notice period

Notification ≥ **10 days** before the end of the notice period applicable to the lease (typically minimum 3-month notice ending on a usual date).

## Reference mortgage rate

Published quarterly by the Office fédéral du logement (OFL / BWO).

A change of **0.25 percentage points** corresponds to:
- **+3 %** rent increase per 0.25 pp rise (for rents below 1.5 % reference)
- **−2.91 %** rent decrease per 0.25 pp fall

Tenant can demand decrease automatically when rate falls.

## Cantonal forms

| Canton | Form provider |
|--------|---------------|
| GE | OCLPF — Office cantonal du logement |
| VD | OCL — Office cantonal du logement |
| ZH | Mietamt der Stadt Zürich (city) / Kanton |
| BE | Wohn- und Mietrecht |
| TI | UDFP cantonale |

Form must be in the language of the canton (FR / DE / IT).

## Common pitfalls

- Increase **null** if cantonal form not used (CO 269d al. 2)
- Reason **must** be clear (one or more of CO 269a a-e)
- Cannot combine reasons retroactively — must be the actual basis
- Tenant has 30 days; if not contested, increase becomes due
